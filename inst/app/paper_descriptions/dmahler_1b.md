Income distributions tend to be more unequal than consumption distributions because incomes can be very low (and even negative) due to negative capital shocks or for other reasons, while subsistence requires daily consumption to be above a minimum level. At the same time, individuals can have very high incomes in a given year but are likely to allocate some of this income for savings or investments rather than consumption. All of this poses a challenge for the comparability of poverty and inequality estimates across countries.
